AMERICA

New York,
The United Sates

Manhattan's Chinatown is home to the highest concentration of Chinese people in the Western Hemisphere, and is also one of the oldest Chinese ethnic enclaves. It is one of nine Chinatown neighborhoods in New York City, but contains the largest ethnic Chinese population outside of Asia.

ASIA

Singapore,
Singapore

Chinatown in Singapore is a sharp contrast to the rest of the city, with low rise buildings and culture bursting out onto the streets, from the fragrant smells of traditional cuisine to the bold red and gold tones that run through the neighbourhood. This age-old precinct is also home to myriad different cultures and is the only Chinatown in the world to boast a Buddhist temple, a mosque and a Hindu temple along a single street.

EUROPE

London,
The United Kingdom

The Chinatown is an ethnic enclave in the borough of Westminster, London, it is one of the oldest Chinese community in Europe. There are many Chinese traditional events, such as during Chinese New Year, which features parades with floats, lion dances and live performances, hundreds of red lanterns during the celebrations make for a spectacular sight.

AUSTRALIA

Melbourne,
Australia

Melbourne's Chinatown is in Little Bourke Street. It is notable as the oldest Chinatown in Australia, the oldest continuous Chinese settlement in Western World, and the longest continuously running Chinatown outside of Asia.

AFRICA

Johannesburg,
South Africa

South Africa has the largest Chinatown and the largest Chinese population of any African country, and remains a popular destination for Chinese immigrants coming to Africa. Derrick Avenue in Cyrildene, Johannesburg, hosts South Africa's largest Chinatown.
